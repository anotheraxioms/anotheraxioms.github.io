Our philosophy

Our passion is building what we call diegetic VR. Creating a world that is full-fledged and thought out is the goal. If we treat it with that respect, it's powerful enough to make everyone believe it. Players should feel immersed, without constant reminders that it's “just a game."
We want it to feel as natural as going out to spending time with friends in an alternate universe. And that's our ultimate objective: to make Another aXiom the "third place" where friends go to hang-out - not just as a game, but as a social community. Social VR can provide that sense of belonging and community in a way that really can’t be done in any other medium.

Employee Bio

In 2016, Oculus Rift launched with Touch. A passionate VR enthusiast since DK2, Lemming, started playing all of the Oculus Touch games and began competing in the VR Challenger League. In the Summer of 2017, a new beta was released for a competitive VR team sport called Echo Arena and caught Lemming's attention. He soon become known for playing the game upside-down and would go on to be the captain of the first two world championships for the game. Two of the original people that worked on Echo Arena, the Davids, would meet Lemming and get to know him and his passion for authentic VR experiences and the zero-G movement mechanic that Echo Arena was built upon where players used their hands to propel themselves through space.
